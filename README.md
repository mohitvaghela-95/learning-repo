# learning-repo

This repository marks the start of a learning journey that I have created for myself with the help of AI, with the purpose of bridging the gap between the skills required in 2026 and my current skillset. This repository will be a store of all the notes, knowledge and information I gather along the way.

## About me and my career

Hello, welcome to the repository. It’s 2026 and I am currently a Senior Data Engineer for Virgin Money. I studied and graduated in Chemistry at the University of Nottingham back in 2016. Wow, I can’t believe it’s been 10 years! That leads me to the reason for this repository.

After graduating, I knew a life in Chemistry wasn't for me. So I learned how to code with Python. I started my career in London back in 2017 with FDM Group, a consultancy that spends weeks training its consultants in a specific role/path and then sends them out to work for its clients. I trained for 10 weeks in Production and Application Support. We powered through different topics, followed by an exam every week. The subjects included:

- Professional Development
- Excel and Visual Basic (Macros)
- Unix/Shell
- Web Development
- JavaScript
- ITIL Foundation (Certification)
- OS Admin
- SQL and PL/SQL

I was a jack of all trades (barely), master of none.

In 2018, I was forced by FDM to move to Newcastle to work for their client and my current employer, Virgin Money, which is now Nationwide Bank. I completed some BI and Data Warehouse upskilling with FDM, then moved to Newcastle. After my contract ended with FDM, Virgin Money offered me a full-time contract and the rest is history.

I work within their Data Warehouse, which follows a star schema model, and I started learning ETL development, SQL and Data Engineering on the job, with help from a colleague/friend/mentor. My progress was great. We had a large team of developers, testers and business analysts, and life was good.

Then Covid-19 turned life upside down and rumours of a lockdown occurred in early 2020, at which point we were all working remotely. I eventually decided to move back home to the Midlands and have been working remotely ever since. It was definitely an adjustment. Since then, most of the teams were made redundant and/or left, as the job market for IT and remote working was booming at the time. A lot of knowledge was lost, including the supportive environment I had in the office. Looking back at it now, this felt like a setback in my career development.

Virgin Money was acquired by Clydesdale & Yorkshire Bank, more people left and there was a shift in culture. All of a sudden, due to a lack of resources, we all had to become designers, testers and developers, which left no time to actually learn and grow.

To cut a long story short, it’s 2026 and I have been working predominantly with Oracle SQL and Oracle Warehouse Builder, an outdated ETL tool with no support. I stopped receiving opportunities to develop my skills, I didn’t really have a mentor or anyone to go to who could help me with the technical side of things, and my work became painfully boring.

I am now in a position in my career and role where I feel behind the market baseline and would like to bridge the gap.

## My motivation

I need a change. I want to move to a job that I enjoy, where I can make a meaningful difference and continue to grow. It's taken me a long time to get here, battling life itself and my personal health, but I am ready to focus on what matters the most. We spend so much time in our lives working, so why should we settle for the uninteresting and unimportant?

## Outcomes

I aim to move to a new organisation where I feel needed. I want to feel part of a team that is happy to share knowledge and where the culture is focused on doing what is right for the organisation.

I want my skills to be marketable.

I would like to ultimately regain the confidence I had in myself and the passion I had for all things nerdy in the very early stages of my career.

Earning more money wouldn't hurt either.
